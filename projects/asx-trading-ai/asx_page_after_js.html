      What are cookies?

      Cookies are small text files containing information that are stored on your computer or device by your web browser when you visit a website. Cookies may be created by the website the user is visiting (first party cookies), or may be set by websites other than the one the user is currently visiting (third party cookies).

      Cookies are widely used to allow a website to recognise a users device, preferences and generally improve the efficiency of a website, as well as observing behaviour and compiling aggregate statistics. A common example is an authentication cookie which confirms whether the user is logged in to an account.

      Cookies that are erased at the end of your browsing session are known as session cookies. Other cookies compile longer-term information about a user and their browsing history and will remain on your computer until you erase them or they are erased by your browser at the end of their expiry date (persistent cookies).

      Most cookies do not contain information that personally identifies a user, but in some circumstances this information may be linked with other information we hold to identify you. For information on how ASX handles your personal information please see our Privacy Statement.

      Use of cookies by ASX

      Strictly Necessary Cookies

      Always Active

      Strictly necessary cookies ensure that our website is able to function properly. They include cookies that help you log in, provide core services and features, help keep our website secure and help us detect fraud or crime. We dont have to ask for your consent to store these cookies on your browser.

      Analytics Cookies

      These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site, and will not be able to monitor its performance.

      Functional Cookies

      These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly.

      Targeting Cookies

      These cookies are used to collect information about a visitors behavior across devices and websites. They are used to allow us to tailor marketing information provided through our partners to audiences who may have an interest in that information, and to measure the effectiveness of our advertising. They include third-party cookies placed by our partners who share cookie data with us.
